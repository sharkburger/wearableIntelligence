## Core framing

> Wearable intelligence should be understood not merely as body-worn sensing or
> AI-enabled computation, but as **perceptual media**: body-worn or body-coupled
> systems that mediate how users perceive their bodies, environments, and
> possibilities for action.

| Element | Contribution |
|---|---|
| **Object** | Wearable intelligence *as perceptual media* |
| **Mechanism** | *Perception-in-the-loop* (sensing → inference → feedback → perceptual recalibration → embodied action → new sensing) |
| **Analytical tool** | *Loop-Depth Taxonomy* (L0 Record → L4 Incorporate) |
| **Design agenda** | *Perceptual Scaffolding* for embodied interaction |

## How to use this set

1. `05_search_protocol.md` is your **methods section** — run the strings, log counts in a PRISMA-style flow, populate `03_coding_scheme.csv` as you screen.
2. `01_review.md` is the **conceptual spine** — it is written to be defensible *now*, but every empirical claim about a specific system should be reconciled with what you actually find during screening.
3. `04_annotated_bibliography.md` is the **starter corpus** — ~60 anchor works grouped by cluster, each with a one-line "why it matters for the argument."
4. Citations marked **[ANCHOR]** are canonical and safe to lean on. Citations marked **[VERIFY]** are from memory and should be confirmed (year/venue) before submission.

## Honest assessment of the central claim

- "**Perceptual media**" is a *strong, defensible synthesis* — it is not a single
  prior term but a convergence of McLuhan (extension), Ihde (embodiment
  relations), Merleau-Ponty/Polanyi (incorporation & transparency), and Gibson
  (perception of action possibilities). Frame it as an integrative construct, not
  a coinage from nothing.
- "**Perception-in-the-loop**" is **not yet an established term**, but it sits in a
  crowded neighborhood (human-in-the-loop, body-in-the-loop, closed-loop
  biofeedback, perception-action cycle, active inference, sensorimotor
  contingencies). It is publishable as a *reframing* that relocates the locus of
  intervention from cognition/supervision (HITL) and signal-regulation
  (biofeedback) to **perception itself**. See §5 and the comparison table for the
  differentiation you must make explicit to reviewers.
- The **loop-depth taxonomy** works, but conflates two axes (feedback
  sophistication vs. degree of bodily incorporation). §6 keeps your 5 levels but
  adds cross-cutting dimensions so reviewers can't collapse it.
