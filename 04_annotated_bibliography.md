# Annotated Bibliography (Starter Corpus)

~60 anchor works, grouped by function in the argument. Each entry: citation →
*why it matters for the perceptual-media thesis*. **[ANCHOR]** = canonical;
**[VERIFY]** = confirm year/venue. Where I am unsure of a detail it is bracketed.

---

## A. Conceptual anchors — mediation, media, incorporation

- **Merleau-Ponty, M. (1945/2012). *Phenomenology of Perception*. [ANCHOR]** — The
  body schema; the blind man's cane and the feather-in-the-hat as incorporation
  into the lived body. *Foundation for "the wearable becomes part of the
  perceiving body" (L4, P4).*
- **Polanyi, M. (1966). *The Tacit Dimension*. [ANCHOR]** — Subsidiary vs. focal
  awareness; we attend *from* tools *to* the world. *Mechanism of transparency:
  why incorporated wearables withdraw from attention.*
- **Ihde, D. (1990). *Technology and the Lifeworld*. [ANCHOR]** — Postphenomenology;
  embodiment / hermeneutic / alterity / background relations. *The single best
  theoretical home for "perceptual media": embodiment relations = perceiving the
  world through the device.*
- **Verbeek, P-P. (2005). *What Things Do*. [ANCHOR]** — Extends Ihde; technological
  mediation of perception and action. *Supports the normative/agency dimension.*
- **McLuhan, M. (1964). *Understanding Media: The Extensions of Man*. [ANCHOR]** —
  Media as extensions (and amputations) of the sensorium. *Licenses the word
  "media" and the extension/reorganization framing of §4.*
- **Clark, A. & Chalmers, D. (1998). The extended mind. *Analysis*. [ANCHOR]** —
  Cognitive extension beyond the skin. *Cousin argument for perceptual extension.*
- **Clark, A. (2003). *Natural-Born Cyborgs*. [ANCHOR]** — Humans as constitutively
  tool-incorporating. *Bridges extended mind to wearables.*

## B. Perception, body schema, enaction

- **Gibson, J.J. (1979). *The Ecological Approach to Visual Perception*. [ANCHOR]** —
  Affordances; perception of *possibilities for action*. *Defines the object
  wearables most distinctively reshape — your "possibilities for action."*
- **Gallagher, S. (2005). *How the Body Shapes the Mind*. [ANCHOR]** — Body schema
  vs. body image. *Precision vocabulary for L3 (perception) vs L4 (body schema).*
- **de Vignemont, F. (2018). *Mind the Body*. [VERIFY]** — Bodily self-awareness,
  body schema. *Supports P4 and the perceptual-change measures (P7).*
- **Head, H. & Holmes, G. (1911). Sensory disturbances from cerebral lesions.
  *Brain*. [VERIFY]** — Origin of "body schema." *Historical anchor.*
- **Varela, F., Thompson, E. & Rosch, E. (1991). *The Embodied Mind*. [ANCHOR]** —
  Enaction; cognition as embodied action. *Grounds perception as enacted, not
  received — why feedback can constitute new perception.*
- **Noë, A. (2004). *Action in Perception*. [ANCHOR]** — Perception is something we
  *do*; sensorimotor knowledge. *Core support for P5 (active exploration).*
- **O'Regan, J.K. & Noë, A. (2001). A sensorimotor account of vision and visual
  consciousness. *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*. [ANCHOR]** — Sensorimotor
  contingencies. *Explains *why* sensory substitution yields genuine perception;
  the science behind P5.*
- **Friston, K. (2010). The free-energy principle. *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*.
  [ANCHOR]** — Active inference / predictive processing. *Explains why
  precision-weighted feedback can retune perception; relate, don't conflate (§5.2).*
- **Clark, A. (2016). *Surfing Uncertainty*. [ANCHOR]** — Accessible predictive-
  processing synthesis. *Bridge from active inference to design.*

## C. Embodied interaction in HCI

- **Dourish, P. (2001). *Where the Action Is*. [ANCHOR]** — Foundational embodied-
  interaction text; Heideggerian ready-to-hand. *Theoretical parent of the design
  agenda; legitimizes the framing within HCI.*
- **Klemmer, S., Hartmann, B. & Takayama, L. (2006). How bodies matter: five themes
  for interaction design. *DIS*. [VERIFY]** — *Practical bridge from phenomenology
  to design themes (thinking through doing, performance, risk).*
- **Höök, K. (2018). *Designing with the Body: Somaesthetic Interaction Design*.
  [ANCHOR]** — Soma design; designing for felt experience. *Methodological basis
  for P2 (awareness) and P7 (lived-experience evaluation).*
- **Höök, K. et al. (2016). Somaesthetic appreciation design. *CHI*. [VERIFY]** —
  *Concrete somaesthetic methods.*
- **Schiphorst, T. (2009). soft(n) / somaesthetics in HCI. *CHI* [VERIFY]** —
  *Early somaesthetic wearable-ish artifacts; supports P2.*
- **Mueller, F. et al. (2020). Next Steps for Human-Computer Integration. *CHI*.
  [VERIFY]** — Integration vs. interaction; body as part of the system. *Closest
  HCI agenda to L4; cite as adjacent framing and differentiate (they center
  integration/agency; you center perception).*
- **Ishii, H. & Ullmer, B. (1997). Tangible bits. *CHI*. [ANCHOR]** — *Lineage of
  body/world-coupled interaction.*
- **Weiser, M. (1991). The computer for the 21st century. *Scientific American*.
  [ANCHOR]** — Ubicomp; calm/invisible computing. *The "withdraw from attention"
  ideal that perceptual media operationalize at the body.*

## D. Wearable computing & wearable AI

- **Mann, S. (1997/1998). Wearable computing / humanistic computing; mediated
  reality. *IEEE Computer*. [ANCHOR]** — *Defines the field; "mediated reality"
  is an early gesture toward perception-mediation — cite as proto-thesis.*
- **Starner, T. et al. (1997). Augmented reality through wearable computing.
  [VERIFY]** — *Baseline "wearable as tool/computer."*
- **Rhodes, B. (1997). The wearable remembrance agent. *ISWC*. [VERIFY]** —
  *Context-aware augmentation of memory; L1 baseline.*
- **Picard, R. (1997). *Affective Computing*. [ANCHOR]** — *Sensing affective state;
  exemplar of "wearable as AI"; perception as input not output.*
- **Li, I., Dey, A. & Forlizzi, J. (2010). A stage-based model of personal
  informatics. *CHI*. [ANCHOR]** — *Defines L1 informational framing; the foil.*
- **Rooksby, J. et al. (2014). Personal tracking as lived informatics. *CHI*.
  [VERIFY]** — *Critiques data-centrism; supports the move beyond accuracy.*
- **Choe, E.K. et al. (2014). Understanding quantified-selfers' practices. *CHI*.
  [VERIFY]** — *Empirical grounding of self-tracking limits.*

## E. Feedback, biofeedback, sensory substitution/augmentation

- **Salmoni, A., Schmidt, R. & Walter, C. (1984). Knowledge of results and motor
  learning. *Psychological Bulletin*. [ANCHOR]** — Guidance hypothesis; feedback
  dependency. *The empirical spine of P1 (scaffold-and-fade).*
- **Sigrist, R. et al. (2013). Augmented visual, auditory, haptic, and multimodal
  feedback in motor learning: a review. *Psychonomic Bulletin & Review*.
  [ANCHOR]** — *The single most important feedback-science citation; concurrent
  vs terminal feedback, modality choice, transfer.*
- **Shull, P.B. & Damian, D.D. (2015). Haptic wearables as sensory replacement,
  sensory augmentation and trainer: a review. *J. NeuroEngineering & Rehab*.
  [ANCHOR]** — *The wearable-haptics review that already grades function
  (replacement/augmentation/trainer) — a precedent for your taxonomy; engage it.*
- **Bach-y-Rita, P. (1969). Vision substitution by tactile image projection.
  *Nature*. [ANCHOR]** — *Origin of sensory substitution (TVSS).*
- **Bach-y-Rita, P. & Kercel, S. (2003). Sensory substitution and the human-machine
  interface. *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*. [VERIFY]** — *Distal attribution;
  the perception (not sensation) claim. Cornerstone L4 case.*
- **Meijer, P. (1992). An experimental system for auditory image representations
  (the vOICe). *IEEE Trans. Biomedical Engineering*. [VERIFY]** — *Visual→auditory
  substitution; long-term perceptual acquisition.*
- **Nagel, S. et al. (2005). Beyond sensory substitution—learning the sixth sense
  (feelSpace). [VERIFY]** — *Sensory *augmentation*; emergence of a new felt sense
  after weeks. Best L4 augmentation exemplar.*
- **Novich, S. & Eagleman, D. (2015). [Sensory substitution VEST]. [VERIFY]** —
  *Wearable vibrotactile substitution at scale; learnability of mappings.*
- **Lieberman, J. & Breazeal, C. (2007). TIKL: wearable vibrotactile feedback suit.
  *IEEE Trans. Robotics*. [VERIFY]** — *Vibrotactile motor learning; L2→L3.*
- **Crowell, H. & Davis, I. (2011). Gait retraining to reduce impact loading.
  *Clinical Biomechanics*. [VERIFY]** — *Real-time biofeedback that changes running
  mechanics and persists; L2–L3 with retention evidence.*

## F. Haptics, EMS, actuated wearables & agency

- **Lopes, P. & Baudisch, P. (2013). Muscle-Propelled Force Feedback. *CHI*.
  [VERIFY]** — *EMS as output; the body actuated by the system.*
- **Lopes, P. et al. (2015). Affordance++. *CHI*. [VERIFY]** — *EMS overlays
  "affordances" onto objects — literally reshaping perceived possibilities for
  action; vivid perceptual-media case (and agency tension).*
- **Lopes, P. et al. (2017/2018). Providing haptics to walls / adding force feedback
  to MR via EMS. [VERIFY]** — *Body-coupled perceptual modification; P3/P4.*
- **Limerick, H., Coyle, D. & Moore, J. (2014). The experience of agency in
  human-computer interactions: a review. *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*.
  [VERIFY]** — *Sense-of-agency framework for HCI; basis of P3 and agency measures.*
- **Coyle, D. et al. (2012). I did that! Measuring users' experience of agency.
  *CHI*. [VERIFY]** — *Intentional binding in HCI; concrete agency measure (P7).*
- **Kilteni, K., Groten, R. & Slater, M. (2012). The sense of embodiment in virtual
  reality. *Presence*. [ANCHOR]** — *Ownership/agency/self-location triad; the
  embodiment measurement model to import for P7.*
- **Botvinick, M. & Cohen, J. (1998). Rubber hands feel touch. *Nature*. [ANCHOR]** —
  *Plasticity of body ownership; bodies/schemas are designable.*

## G. Rehabilitation, exosuits, prosthesis embodiment

- **Zhang, J. et al. (2017). Human-in-the-loop optimization of exoskeleton
  assistance during walking. *Science*. [ANCHOR]** — *Key CONTRAST: body-in-loop
  optimization of *physiology*, not perception; sharpens §5.2.*
- **Awad, L. et al. (2017). Soft exosuit for post-stroke gait. *Science
  Translational Medicine*. [VERIFY]** — *L2–L4 assistive case; incorporation
  questions.*
- **Marasco, P. et al. (2018). Illusory movement perception improves motor control
  for prosthetic hands. *Science Translational Medicine*. [VERIFY]** — *Feedback
  that creates *perception* (kinesthetic illusion) and improves control — exact
  perceptual-media logic.*
- **Makin, T., de Vignemont, F. & Faisal, A. (2017). Neurocognitive barriers to the
  embodiment of technology. *Nature Biomedical Engineering*. [ANCHOR]** —
  *Conditions and failure modes for incorporation; the science behind P4 and the
  L3→L4 boundary.*

## H. AR/XR & smart textiles as perceptual interfaces

- **Slater, M. & Sanchez-Vives, M. (2016). Enhancing our lives with immersive VR.
  *Frontiers in Robotics and AI*. [VERIFY]** — *Body ownership/illusion in XR;
  perceptual reorganization.*
- **Poupyrev, I. et al. (2016). Project Jacquard: interactive digital textiles at
  scale. *CHI*. [VERIFY]** — *Smart-textile interaction; body-coupled input
  surface (mostly L1–L2, useful boundary case).*
- **Devendorf, L. et al. (2016). "I don't want to wear a screen": probing textile
  displays. *CHI*. [VERIFY]** — *Critiques screen-on-body; argues for
  textile/perceptual qualities — supports P2/P6.*
- **Perner-Wilson, H., Buechley, L. & Satomi, M. (2011). Handcrafting textile
  interfaces. *TEI*. [VERIFY]** — *Material/embodied perspective on wearables.*

## I. Review-method anchors

- **Whittemore, R. & Knafl, K. (2005). The integrative review: updated
  methodology. *J. Advanced Nursing*. [VERIFY]** — *Justifies the integrative
  (vs. systematic-aggregative) genre.*
- **Torraco, R. (2005). Writing integrative literature reviews. *Human Resource
  Development Review*. [VERIFY]** — *Integrative review as theory building.*
- **Page, M. et al. (2021). PRISMA 2020. *BMJ*. [VERIFY]** — *For the screening-flow
  diagram in the systems corpus.*
